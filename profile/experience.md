As a Research Assistant at the AI Caring Institute working with Professor Zhi Tan, I contributed to the development of an ambient reminder system for individuals with mild cognitive impairment who often struggle to remember daily tasks. The goal was to build a system that could deliver reminders at the right moment by combining natural language interaction with data from in home sensors and a human activity recognition system. I built the complete backend and frontend for this study, including the full pipeline that supported reminder authoring, intent extraction, sensor and activity mapping and trigger code generation. Our early approach relied on an event based model where reminders were authored through a structured form and the system mapped user instructions to sensor events and activities. I collaborated with researchers at Georgia Tech to deploy and demonstrate this system in the Aware Home environment. This work went through NSF review and received funding to continue the project. During Study 1 I also explored how to represent sensor locations and activity semantics so that an LLM could reason about the layout of a home. I worked on mapping sensors to spatial locations to keep HAR output and reminder logic aligned and I developed a feasibility gate that used a reasoning model and a house graph to determine whether a requested reminder condition was possible in a given home environment.

During iterative testing we discovered that using a single large language model to fill the form was unreliable because the model struggled to infer the correct combination of activities and sensors from the user description. This led us to redesign the pipeline into multiple stages: one model focused on chatting with the user and asking the right questions, one extracted intent and represented it in a structured format and another selected the appropriate activities and sensors. We eventually moved beyond template based representations and adopted a code generation approach for producing the trigger logic of a reminder since real reminders often require combinations of time, sensor conditions, activities or sequential patterns. I used LangChain with different models for each stage and supplied the models with domain specific context such as the list of available sensors, supported activities, user routines and time information. I also worked on designing the conversational assistant so it could guide users through reminder creation in a natural and efficient way. We ran two user studies on Prolific and submitted a paper based on the results.

## Northeastern University, AI-CARING Institute (Graduate Research Assistant, Feb 2024 – Dec 2025)

At the AI-CARING Institute, I helped build an ambient reminder system for people with mild cognitive impairment. The goal was to take a natural conversation like “remind me to do X when Y happens” and turn it into a working reminder that triggers at the right moment using in-home sensor signals and activity recognition outputs.

I built a multi-stage LLM workflow that turns caregiver dialogue into structured reminder intent and executable trigger logic. To make the system safe and reliable, I added guardrails around code generation: the generated Python trigger functions were validated to enforce a safe structure, block disallowed imports, and ensure they compile cleanly before they run.

I also owned the real-time event pipeline. I subscribed to AWS IoT Core over MQTT, normalized sensor and activity messages from 120+ sensors at around 3,000 events per second, and forwarded them over gRPC to the reminder executor.

To measure quality and drive iteration, I evaluated system outputs across two user studies and tracked how often reminders were correct. Across iterations, correct outputs improved from 38.6% to 88.3%, and incorrect outputs dropped to 0%.
